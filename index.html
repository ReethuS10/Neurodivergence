Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the biological process through which green plants, algae, and certain bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy. This process occurs primarily in the chloroplasts of plant cells, which contain a pigment called chlorophyll. Chlorophyll absorbs sunlight, particularly in the blue and red wavelengths, and uses that energy to drive a series of chemical reactions.

During photosynthesis, plants take in carbon dioxide from the atmosphere through tiny openings in their leaves called stomata. At the same time, water is absorbed by the roots from the soil and transported to the leaves through specialized vascular tissues. Using energy from sunlight, these substances are transformed into glucose, a simple sugar that plants use as a source of energy and growth.

Oxygen is produced as a by-product of this reaction and is released into the atmosphere. This oxygen is essential for the survival of most living organisms on Earth because it supports cellular respiration. In this way, photosynthesis plays a critical role in maintaining the balance of oxygen and carbon dioxide in the planet’s atmosphere.

In addition to providing oxygen, photosynthesis forms the foundation of most food chains. Plants store the chemical energy they produce, and when animals eat plants, that energy is transferred through the ecosystem. Therefore, photosynthesis not only supports plant life but also sustains nearly all forms of life on Earth.

Plants use sunlight to make their own food. This process is called photosynthesis. It happens mainly in the green leaves of the plant. A green substance called chlorophyll acts like a sponge to catch the sunlight.

Plants breathe in carbon dioxide from the air. Their roots drink water from the soil. Using the trapped sunlight, the plant turns the air and water into sugar. The plant uses this sugar as food to grow strong.

While making this food, the plant breathes out oxygen into the air. All people and animals need to breathe this oxygen to stay alive.

Because plants make their own food, they are the base of the food chain. When animals eat plants, they get this energy. So, photosynthesis is what keeps almost all life on Earth alive and breathing.
